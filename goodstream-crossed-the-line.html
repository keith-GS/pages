What They're Asking For

The roadmap is now written by customers.

Every ask below came from an actual user using the actual product. This is the single most valuable list we've ever produced. Click any item to expand.

A note on the italicized lines inside each expanded card: these paraphrase the spirit of what each customer asked for, drawn from the onboarding and connection calls. Direct, verbatim quotes appear only in the main quote wall above.

DataTribe / Matt Moran
Interactive liquidation waterfall visualization

Matt wants the preference stack and waterfall to be a clickable, dynamic view: change an exit price, see who gets what, see coverage ratios, convert/non-convert toggles, etc. This is the single highest-value feature request from the DataTribe call.

"If you could get a visual on the waterfall where I can just drag and see what happens at different exit values, that would be incredible."

This is also a natural extension of the data we already extract. The preference terms, liquidation multiples, and participation caps are already normalized in the model - we just haven't built the interactive scenario UI on top yet.

● High priority Est: post-GA sprint 1-2 Scope: medium
Flashpoint / Daniil Kuzovkin
Multi-folder Box import during onboarding

Flashpoint stores portfolio-company docs in separate top-level Box folders per fund and per strategy. Today our Box integration takes one folder at a time. They want to select several folders in one go during first-run ingestion.

"We have 60 portfolio companies, but they're split across different funds in Box. Can we connect three folders at once?"

Technically small - the ingestion worker already supports multi-root collections. This is mostly UI plumbing on the Box connector setup flow.

● High priority Est: this sprint Scope: small
DataTribe / Matt Moran
Notes and annotations on portfolio companies

A lightweight notes layer attached to each company: meeting takeaways, board observations, quick qualitative context that doesn't live in a document. Matt specifically wants to be able to append a short note after a 1:1 with a founder.

"It would be great to have a place to drop notes - something like 'talked to the CEO today, here's what I learned' - that lives with the company record."
● Medium priority Est: sprint 2 Scope: small
Flashpoint / Tatiana Rozhko
EU-resident data warehouse option

Flashpoint has LPs with EU data-residency requirements. They asked whether we can offer an EU-hosted storage/processing option for firms with that constraint.

"Some of our LPs will want to see EU data residency on the contract. Is that something you can do?"

This is real procurement math. EU residency will gate a meaningful slice of European LP-driven buyers. Worth scoping sooner than later, even if we sell it as a paid tier.

● Medium priority Est: post-GA roadmap Scope: large
DataTribe / Matt Moran
Custom fields on the company record

DataTribe tracks cybersecurity-specific fields that aren't in our base schema: threat-model segment, security-clearance status of key hires, specific compliance motions (FedRAMP trajectory, etc.). They want a custom-fields feature per tenant.

"We have a few things we like to track that are specific to cyber - can we add our own fields?"
● Medium priority Est: sprint 2-3 Scope: medium
Flashpoint / Daniil Kuzovkin
Webhooks and outbound API for journal events

Flashpoint wants to push portfolio changes into their existing internal stack: send a webhook when a new SAFE is extracted, when a valuation changes, when a key term is updated. They've already built downstream workflows they want to wire up.

"If you can fire a webhook whenever something updates, we can plug that straight into our systems."
● Medium priority Est: post-GA Scope: small-medium
Both / enterprise ask
Role-based access control for deal team vs. back office

Both firms want finer permissions - the deal team sees everything, the back office may only see financial terms, compliance sees audit trail and extraction provenance only. Standard enterprise ask, and increasingly a requirement rather than a nice-to-have.

"Daniil shouldn't be able to accidentally overwrite something the back office owns. Role separation matters to us."
● Medium priority Est: post-GA sprint 2 Scope: medium
DataTribe / Kellee Griffin
Bulk document upload with auto-classification

Kellee has the entire historical portfolio - messy, mixed, a lot of side-letter PDFs alongside SAFEs and stock purchase agreements - and she wants to drag-select 200 files into one window and let us sort it out. We do some of this today. She wants more transparency into what got classified as what.

"If I could just throw everything at it and see the classification step before it commits, that would make onboarding a one-day thing."
● Medium priority Est: sprint 1-2 Scope: medium
The Hard Stuff

What's not working yet. What we heard and what we're doing.

No sandbagging. These are the real rough edges our first customers pointed at, along with what we've already put in motion. Investors and employees should both see this list - the real signal of maturity is whether we're honest about friction.

The italicized lines inside each expanded card paraphrase the spirit of the feedback given on the call. Verbatim quotes live in the main quote wall above.

High severity / Flashpoint
Box integration hit an OAuth-scope rough edge mid-onboarding

During Flashpoint's initial Box connect, we tripped on an OAuth scope configuration and had to fall back to a manual folder upload for the first pass. Not a great first impression for a customer whose primary file system is Box.

"It took us a few tries to get Box to behave - that's one of the things we'll want smooth before rolling this to the rest of the team."
In motion: Box scope mapping was corrected during the call. Added to pre-onboarding checklist so every subsequent Box customer gets a validated connection before the first live session. Engineering is also adding an observability hook so we see scope failures in real time rather than discovering them on a customer call.
Medium severity / Flashpoint
Duplicate documents created confusion in the initial ingestion

Flashpoint's Box had multiple near-identical copies of the same underlying document (clean version, signed version, redline, etc.). Our deduplication logic surfaced all of them without a clear primary-vs-variant signal, which made the first review pass noisier than it needed to be.

"We had the same SAFE three times - clean, signed, executed - and your system pulled all three. That's not wrong, but we had to think about which one is the real one."
In motion: Ranking "executed and signed" as primary with prior versions as linked variants, not duplicates. Engineering ticket in flight; expected in the next release cut.
Medium severity / DataTribe
Historical documents are "the messiest" and extraction quality varied

Matt was candid that DataTribe's historical document set is inconsistent - scanned PDFs, DocuSign envelopes, inline-term emails. Our extraction did well on the clean, born-digital docs and less well on the pre-2020 scans. He flagged this fairly, as ground truth rather than as a complaint.

"I think the materials provided historically have been the messiest. The clean stuff looks awesome. The old scanned stuff - I can see where you had trouble."
In motion: Adding an OCR-quality confidence indicator in the document viewer so users see immediately when a source scan is low-confidence and know to validate the extracted terms. Longer-term: a curated model pass specifically tuned for pre-2018 scanned SAFE / NVCA legacy docs.
Medium severity / DataTribe
OAuth scope concerns for the Google Workspace connector

Matt pushed appropriately hard on the Google Drive integration: what scopes are we requesting, who sees what, and what's the blast radius if credentials are compromised. Good pressure, and legitimately something we want to be able to answer without pausing.

"Walk me through exactly what scopes you're asking for and what the failure mode looks like if someone's token gets leaked."
In motion: Writing a customer-facing security one-pager specifically on connector scopes and failure modes, so this conversation becomes "here's the doc" instead of "let me get back to you." Ties into the broader SOC 2 Type II audit work already in progress.
Medium severity / both
Naming and terminology isn't always firm-native

Both firms use their own internal vocabulary for things - what we call a "position" Flashpoint calls an "investment"; DataTribe's "company" is our "portfolio entity." Our labels are reasonable, but they're not their labels. Small friction, but it accumulates.

"We call these investments, not positions. It's a small thing, but every time I see the word 'position' I have to translate."
In motion: Evaluating tenant-level terminology overrides as a lightweight customization, probably in the same sprint as custom fields. Not a UI overhaul - a vocabulary map.
Medium / honest acknowledgment
SOC 2 Type II audit is in progress, not complete

Both firms asked about SOC 2 status. We're in the observation period with controls implemented and operating - not yet Type II attested. We were straight about that and neither firm bounced, but both flagged that the final letter is needed before broader internal rollout.

"We trust you on this - our internal compliance team is going to want the attestation letter before we go wall-to-wall."
In motion: SOC 2 Type II audit in progress, observation window runs through the quarter. LLMs will never train on customer data - that's a hard contractual and architectural commitment, not a policy promise. Targeting attestation completion in line with the GA rollout plan.

The work ahead is harder and better than the work behind.

For the first time in GoodStream's history, our roadmap is being shaped by people actually using the product rather than people evaluating it. That's the only version of product development that ever compounds. Thanks for being part of this one.
